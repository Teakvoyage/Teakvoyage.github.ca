International Women's Day

What Is It?

International Women's Day is celebrated on March 8. It is celebrated around the globe to recognize and celebrate women's and girl's social, economic, cultural and political achievements. It is to raise awareness of progress to achieve gender equality. It is a day of unity, celebration, reflection, advocacy and action to achieve a better, fairer world. Gender equality is crucial for prosperous economies. This day helps face those challenges.

Problems To Solve

  • Investing in women
    • Gender equality is a huge human rights issue. It is a problem we need to solve.
  • Ending Poverty
    • Due to COVID and conflicts, 75 million more people have fallen into severe poverty since 2020. If immediate action is not taken, over 342 million more women and girls will fall into poverty by 2030.
  • Creating a gender responsive financing
    • Conflicts and rising prices may lead to 75% of countries cutting public spending by 2025, meaning it impacts women and their essential services.
  • Supporting change makers
    • Feminist organizations receive only 0.13% of official development assistance.

Theme

The theme for 2024 is "Invest in women: Accelerate progress", which will highlight the importance of gender-equality measures. This is to help spread awareness on gender inequality and problems which will arise in the future, therefore we need to solve them now by gathering attention.
