BLACK HISTORY 



	What Is Black History Month?

		Black History Month is an annual celebration
		of African American achievements and their
		roles in History. Since 1976, February has
		been designated as Black History Month.
		It honors the contributions in society, 
		politics, science, culture and more.
		This event was the result of a historian
		named Carter G. Woodson. It had been created
		to raise awareness to Black identity.




	What Are Themes?

		Black History Month has themes which it
		follows to recognize certain areas each 
		year. It guides the experience and highlighs
		important contributions.
