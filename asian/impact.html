IMPACTS OF
ASIAN IMMIGRATION

Economic Influence

Sikhs from Punjab, India came to British Columbia to work in the lumber, mining, and railway industries, and later in agriculture. The Chinese also contributed greatly to the economy with labour and building for cheap like the Canadian Pacific Railway. The Japanese had lots of fishing boats. Vietnamese Canadians work in many different sectors of Canada's economy, notably manufacturing and scientific and technical occupations. Many Vietnamese Canadians are also entrepreneurs, owning and operating businesses such as restaurants and grocery stores.

Cultural Influence

Since 1900, Chinese Canadians have settled primarily in urban areas, particularly in Vancouver and Toronto. They have contributed to every aspect of Canadian society, from literature to sports, politics to civil rights, film to music, business to philanthropy, and education to religion. This includes many restaurants like in Chinatown which is a well known thing. Vietnamese cuisine is popular throughout Canada from things like pho. The Indian influence on Canadian culture can be seen in various aspects, including food, language, music, fashion, and traditions. There are many Indian restaurants. Japanese food is also very popular.

Challenges and Achievements

The Chinese faced much discrimination throughout the years. Initially, they were faced with the Head Tax, then the Immigration Act, both systemic forms to stop Chinese Immigration. They were also seen as enemy aliens and were payed significantly less. Through efforts, they were able to achieve better rights and abolish these acts. South Asians were discriminated against too with the Continuous Journey Act. They were thought to be stealing jobs. They greatly contributed to the economy. The Japanese were interned and deported. They were able to achieve their rights back and advocated for justice. The Vietnamese initially came due to being refugees. They contributed a lot to society through economic prosperity.
